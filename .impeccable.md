## Design Context

### Product
DreamySuite — a SaaS platform for creating beautiful event sites. While rooted in weddings, the platform handles any milestone event: anniversaries, celebrations, engagements, and beyond. Couples and event organizers use it to build sites featuring countdowns, RSVP, photo galleries, and cinematic entrance animations.

### Users
Couples and event organizers planning a milestone event. They are emotionally invested — this is one of the most important days of their lives. Context: leisurely planning sessions (morning coffee, laptop at home), not rushed. They arrive excited and creative, not anxious or under pressure. Primary job: build a beautiful event site quickly without it feeling like work.

### Brand Personality
**Cinematic · Effortless · Expressive**

The feeling of a beautifully shot wedding film — emotional depth, rich texture, unhurried — delivered through a tool that's actually fast and intuitive. DreamySuite should make every other event tool look boring without making users feel like they're doing work.

Anti-references: Typical wedding pink/floral SaaS. Template-heavy Canva-style tools. Squarespace beige. Anything that screams "wedding clip art" or "stock photo couple laughing".

References: A24 film aesthetics. Kinfolk magazine. The tension between editorial restraint and cinematic warmth. Awwwards SOTD editorial portfolios.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light — warm cream (`oklch(97% 0.01 80)`) surfaces, not clinical white. Couples use this during daytime planning sessions.
- **Accent**: Warm gold / amber (`oklch(72% 0.14 68)`). The current teal (#0d9488) should be replaced — it reads as generic SaaS, not milestone event.
- **Typography**: High-contrast editorial pairing — large display serif or variable-weight face for headings, refined geometric sans for UI text. NOT wedding script fonts. NOT Inter.
- **Texture**: Subtle film grain on fixed backgrounds (pseudo-element, pointer-events: none, opacity ≤ 0.04).
- **Motion**: Slow, weighted transitions with custom cubic-bezier. Spring physics on hover. Nothing snappy, bouncy, or instant.
- **Layout**: Asymmetric with breathing room. Not every item in a card. Not identical grids. Space is intentional.

### Design Principles
1. **Cinematic restraint** — Every element earns its place. Whitespace is not empty, it's intentional tension.
2. **Effortless flow** — The interface should feel lighter than its feature set. Complexity is revealed progressively, not displayed all at once.
3. **Emotional resonance** — This tool exists for one of the biggest days of someone's life. The interface should honor that weight without being heavy-handed about it.
4. **Expressive without effort** — Users should feel like designers, not operators filling out a form.
5. **Platform breadth** — Design decisions should work for weddings AND other milestone events without either feeling like an afterthought.
